Scholarship, Bursary & Discount Help

About Scholarship, Bursary & Discount

The Scholarship, Bursary & Discount module allows you to manage financial assistance programs for students. This feature enables you to apply different types of financial credits to student accounts, helping to track scholarships, bursaries, and discounts offered by your institution.

Financial assistance can be applied to individual students or groups of students, and can be set for specific academic years and terms.

How to Apply a Scholarship, Bursary or Discount

Navigate to Headquarters > Scholarship, Bursary & Discount from the main menu.

Select one or more students from the student list by checking the boxes next to their names.

Click the "Apply Credit" button to open the credit application form.

Select the credit type (Scholarship, Bursary, or Discount) from the dropdown menu.

Enter the amount or percentage value for the credit.

Select the applicable academic year and term.

Add any notes or references if needed.

Click "Submit" to apply the credit to the selected students.

Viewing Applied Credits

To view credits that have already been applied to students:

Navigate to Headquarters > Scholarship, Bursary & Discount.

Use the search function to find a specific student or browse the list.

Click on a student's name to view their credit history.

The credit history will show all applied scholarships, bursaries, and discounts with details.

Modifying or Removing Credits

To edit or remove an applied credit:

Navigate to the student's credit history as described above.

Locate the credit entry you wish to modify.

Click the edit icon (pencil) to modify the credit details.

Click the delete icon (trash) to remove the credit (you will be asked to confirm).

Make your changes and click "Save" to apply modifications.

Important Notes

  • Credits applied will automatically adjust the student's fee balance.
  • Some credits may have expiration dates or specific terms of use.
  • System administrators can set up credit types with specific rules and limitations.
  • All credit applications are logged for audit purposes.

Configuration Options

Administrators can configure various aspects of the financial assistance system:

Feature Description Access Level
Credit Types Define different types of financial assistance (e.g., Merit Scholarship, Need-Based Bursary, Sibling Discount) Administrator
Amount Rules Set fixed amounts, percentages, or formulas for calculating financial assistance Administrator
Eligibility Criteria Define conditions that students must meet to qualify for specific financial assistance Administrator
Application Limits Set maximum amounts or usage limits for different credit types Administrator
Approval Workflows Configure multi-level approval processes for financial assistance applications Administrator
